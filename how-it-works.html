How it works

Architecture &
data flow.

The device never talks to the public internet for usage data. Your laptop runs a broker that exposes a small JSON endpoint; the device polls it over your LAN.

Open-Meteo

Weather, sunrise/sunset

The only public-internet calls the device makes are SNTP for time and a once-an-hour weather fetch. Usage telemetry never leaves your LAN.

Properties

Designed for trust.

No cloud account for the device

The device itself doesn't sign in anywhere. Anthropic / OpenAI / Google credentials live on your laptop with the respective CLI.

HMAC-SHA256 + AES-CTR

Every request from device → broker carries an HMAC over the timestamp + body. Payloads are AES-CTR encrypted with a per-device PSK.

Memorable passphrase, derived key

You pick a passphrase at setup. PSK is derived locally — passphrase never travels to the device in clear, and never leaves your machine over the LAN.

Per-device control plane

Rotate keys, switch theme, change broker URL — all from Claude Code, Codex or Gemini via the /cwallmonitor:configure skill.

On the wire

What the device actually receives.

One poll, one JSON object. The broker aggregates whatever the CLIs are writing locally and serves it shaped the way the firmware expects.

The cards array is positional: 0 = Current session, 1 = Weekly, 2 = Design (optional smaller card).
